BPC-157 (5mg)

Format: Lyophilized Powder (Body Protection Compound-157)

🧬 Mechanism of Action

A synthetic peptide sequence derived from a protective protein naturally found in human gastric juice. Renowned for its potent angiogenic (blood vessel growth) and tissue-healing properties.

  • Accelerates the upregulation of growth factors to repair tendons, ligaments, and muscle tissue.
  • Promotes fibroblast migration and collagen synthesis at injury sites.
  • Exhibits systemic anti-inflammatory actions and supports mucosal lining repair.

🧪 Reconstitution Guide

  • Diluent: Add 2.0 mL of Bacteriostatic (BAC) Water gently down the side of the vial.
  • Mixing: Do not shake. Swirl gently until the solution is completely clear.
  • Final Concentration: 2,500 mcg (2.5mg) per 1 mL (equivalent to 25 mcg per 1 syringe unit).
  • Storage: Store in refrigerator at 36°F–46°F (2°C–8°C). Stable after mixing, but avoid excessive shaking.

📅 Protocol & Dosing

Frequency: Subcutaneous injection administered 1 to 2 times daily. Unlike GHRHs, timing relative to food intake is flexible.

Targeting: Can be injected systemically (belly fat) as it spreads throughout the body, though some protocols prefer injecting near the site of localized joint or tissue injury.

⚠️ Safety & Side Effects

  • Common: Generally well-tolerated. Occasional mild injection site redness, transient dizziness immediately post-injection, or mild digestive changes.

CRITICAL WARNINGS:

  • Discontinue use if unusual localized swelling or unmanageable joint irritation occurs.
  • Typically run in cycles of 4–12 weeks followed by a rest period.
  • For laboratory research use only.
