Retatrutide (30mg)

Format: Lyophilized Powder

🧬 Mechanism of Action

Triple agonist targeting GLP-1, GIP, and Glucagon receptors.

  • Enhances insulin secretion & delays gastric emptying.
  • Suppresses appetite.
  • Upregulates energy expenditure and lipid (fat) metabolism.

🧪 Reconstitution Guide

  • Diluent: Add 3.0 mL of Bacteriostatic (BAC) Water gently down the side of the vial.
  • Mixing: Do not shake. Swirl gently until the solution is completely clear.
  • Final Concentration: 10mg per 1 mL (1mg per 10 units on a standard U-100 syringe).
  • Storage: Store in refrigerator at 36°F–46°F (2°C–8°C).

📅 Protocol & Titration

Frequency: Subcutaneous injection once weekly (every 7 days).

Timeline Weekly Dose Syringe Volume (U-100)
Weeks 1–4 2.0 mg 20 units
Weeks 5–8 4.0 mg 40 units
Weeks 9–12 8.0 mg 80 units
Week 13+ 12.0 mg (Max) 120 units (Requires 2 shots)

⚠️ Safety & Side Effects

  • Common: Nausea, diarrhea, vomiting, constipation, decreased appetite, mild increase in resting heart rate, and transient injection site irritation.

CRITICAL WARNINGS:

  • Monitor resting heart rate regularly.
  • Discontinue immediately if severe abdominal pain occurs.
  • For laboratory research use only.
